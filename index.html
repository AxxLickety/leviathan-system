Leviathan — Regime-Aware Housing Risk Research

Leviathan tests whether housing affordability stress behaves like a regime switch rather than a continuous predictor. The core finding: affordability conditions downside risk severity rather than predicting returns — it functions as a gating mechanism, not an alpha signal.

Core result. In adverse real-rate regimes, the DTI-based fragility filter identifies periods of materially elevated crash probability. The signal behaves like a binary gate (reduce exposure / stay invested) rather than a continuous dial. DTI does not add stable incremental signal beyond the regime itself.
Out-of-sample validation (Phase OOS, pre-registered design). Train window: 1990Q1–2007Q4. Test window: 2008Q1–present. All thresholds frozen before the test window was opened. Results on the test window:
  • Regime overlay max drawdown: −0.046 vs always-in −0.318
  • Overlay p05: −0.003 vs always-in −0.067
  • Fragile regime conditional crash frequency CI does not overlap stable regime CI
All three pre-specified success criteria met. Limitation: 48 test-window quarters, sparse crash events — conclusions are directional, not definitive.

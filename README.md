# 🐳 Leviathan

**A regime-aware downside risk overlay.**

Leviathan does not predict returns.  
It decides **when it’s dangerous to stay invested.**

---

## What It Does

Leviathan reduces exposure when macro conditions become structurally adverse.

> Instead of asking *“what will go up?”*  
> it asks *“when should we not be exposed?”*

---

## Core Result

Across multiple synthetic worlds:

- ✔ Improves downside (p05) in **~90%+** of cases  
- ✔ Reduces max drawdown in **~80–100%** of cases  
- ⚠ Sharpe improvement is **conditional**, not guaranteed  

→ **Reliable protection, not universal alpha**

---

## Default Policy

> **50% de-risk after 2 consecutive quarters of adverse regime**

This avoids overreacting to noise while still responding to sustained risk.

---

## What This Means

Leviathan is:

- ✔ a **risk overlay**
- ✔ a **portfolio-level control tool**
- ✔ a way to **avoid structural fragility**

Leviathan is NOT:

- ❌ a stock-picking model  
- ❌ a return predictor  
- ❌ a standalone strategy  

---

## How to Use
Portfolio = Base Strategy × Leviathan


Use it to:

- reduce exposure during fragile macro regimes  
- control drawdowns in systematic strategies  
- stabilize risk across cycles  

---

## Key Insight

> **Downside risk is driven by macro regime — not valuation signals like DTI.**

DTI-based signals failed to add stable value.  
Regime-based filtering remained robust across all tests.

---

## Positioning

Leviathan is best viewed as:

> **a risk budgeting layer, not an alpha generator**

It trades some upside for **consistent downside protection**.

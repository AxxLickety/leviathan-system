Leviathan • Research Evolution

What survived.

Leviathan began as a broader housing fragility hypothesis. After repeated out-of-sample testing, robustness checks, adversarial worlds, and policy experiments, the model was reduced to its only defensible core.
Leviathan is what remains after everything else failed.
← Back to project overview

1. Thesis

The original thesis was that housing downside risk might be jointly driven by macro regime and affordability stress.

2. What failed

DTI-based signals did not survive fairness and robustness testing. Absolute DTI failed under drift, rolling-percentile DTI failed under cleaner definitions, and even causal joint-trigger worlds did not produce stable incremental value beyond regime.

3. What survived

A regime-only overlay repeatedly improved downside outcomes across single-market, multi-market, adversarial, and positive-drift synthetic worlds. The signal that remained was simple: reduce exposure under adverse macro conditions.

4. What that means

Leviathan is not a return-prediction engine and not a standalone alpha model. It is best understood as a downside-risk overlay: a tool that trades some upside participation for more stable left-tail protection.

5. Why it matters

The final model is intentionally narrow, auditable, and configurable. It answers a practical portfolio question: when does staying fully exposed become a bad bet?

Final model form: mandate-aware regime risk overlay
Default policy: 50% de-risk after 2 consecutive adverse quarters
